If you've been following us , it’s likely that you're familiar with the evolving dynamics of the consumer purchase journey and how these dynamics are enhancing the significance of digital channels throughout the purchase journey. Infact digital ad spend is already higher than traditional channels in UAE.

Now, ecommerce apps are becoming important places for product discovery. Thus, in-app advertising will slowly emerge as a significant strategy as ad spend typically follows consumer eyeballs. These in-app ads will help not only in grabbing user attention but also in boosting overall revenue. However, are there are there any tailwinds for in-app ads in MENA, and can it truly be considered the secret ingredient for enhancing ecommerce profitability?

Read on to find out more.
Advertising is increasingly mobile-first, with in-app advertising gaining prominence
Initially limited to traditional mediums, advertisements are now becoming increasingly digital. Advertisement dollars typically follow consumer dwell time. With consumers becoming increasingly mobile-first, in-app advertising is gaining prominence.
We have seen this play out in USA; wherein the in-app ad revenue penetration is significant
In-app advertising is gaining ground, evident from Amazon's substantial $39.45 billion advertising revenue in 2023 in USA.
Also, ad revenue has become a secret sauce for profitability; Without it, Amazon would have experienced a loss rather than a profit in Q1’24
Further, ads have already become the secret sauce for profitability.

Amazon’s global in-app ads revenue grew by 20% to ~$12bn quarterly or roughly 10% of their retail revenues (excl. Amazon Web Services or AWS. Their retail operating margins (excl. AWS) were roughly 5%. Without in-app ads, Amazon’s retail business would have generated an operating loss of $6bn this quarter instead of a profit of $6bn which it declared.
There are tailwinds already for in-app ads in MENA; customers are increasing their dwell time and discovery on Ecom apps
The potential in MENA's in-app advertising remains largely untapped. With consumer dwell time on e-commerce apps increasing by 5-10%, the significance of these channels in driving product discovery presents a significant opportunity. Moreover, the potential of in-app ads to tap into community-driven niche marketing further opens up an innovative avenue for reaching targeted audiences.
In conclusion, the rise of in-app advertising is transforming the landscape of digital marketing, with significant implications for companies' profitability and consumer engagement. As seen with Amazon's substantial revenue growth driven by in-app ads, this trend is not only limited to the USA but also holds promise in regions like MENA where e-commerce app usage is on the rise. The potential for targeted advertising and community-driven marketing within apps presents an exciting opportunity for businesses to connect with their audiences in innovative ways. As we delve deeper into the MENA in-app market in the next newsletter, we anticipate uncovering more insights.
